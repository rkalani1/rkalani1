I build **open, vendor-neutral public demos and research utilities** for evidence review, methods work, and clinical-tool prototyping. Everything published here is browser-only, no-PHI, MIT-licensed where applicable, and meant to be inspectable.

> **For clinicians.** Public tools here are synthetic/demo/reference surfaces. They are **not medical advice**, **not official systems**, and **not endorsed by any organization**. Do not enter PHI, patient identifiers, learner records, confidential data, or restricted research data.

## What I work on

- **Acute stroke care** — ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke, hyperacute imaging selection, thrombolysis, thrombectomy, monitoring, and transfer coordination.
- **Access-modeling methods** — EVT access modeling, public-reference mapping, and synthetic network simulation.
- **Clinical research** — observational studies, RCT design, evidence synthesis, grant writing, and manuscript preparation.
- **Decision-support tooling** — browser-only, offline-capable, local-data PWAs that respect privacy and review workflows.
- **AI-augmented practice** — pragmatic patterns for clinicians and researchers using LLMs without overclaiming what they can do.

---

## Approach

**Tools are open and local-first.** No backend, no analytics, and no PHI should ever be entered. Public GitHub Pages deployments are not approved clinical, patient-intake, learner-record, research-participant, or operational systems.

**Evidence-anchored.** Clinical recommendations trace to primary citations where applicable. Trial eligibility logic is testable and reviewable.

**Designed for review and adaptation.** Mobile-first, static, auditable, and easy to fork into an approved environment when real-world use is appropriate.
